Slimes


Slimes are gelatinous, ball-shaped, adorable alien creatures that live on the far away planet called the Far, Far Range. There are several different species of slimes, each found in certain locations on the Far, Far Range. Though their most common features are the permanent happy expressions they have on their faces when fed and their high pitched voices, each type of slime has a unique trait that is used to identify it.

Docile Slimes


These Slimes cannot harm you under normal conditions unless feral.

Pink Slime


Pink Slimes are the most common slime found on the Far, Far Range. They're cheerful, docile, and the easiest of all slimes to ranch. A pink slime will eat anything you put in front of it.

Tabby Slime


Tabby Slimes, or Tabbies for short, are one of the most common slimes in Slime Rancher. They are easily identified by their resemblance to domesticated cats, as well as their behavior and dietary requirement of Meat.

Phosphor Slime


The Phosphor Slime is a nocturnal slime easily identified by its violet coloration, wings, and soft bright glow. If it is exposed to sunlight for too long they disappear.

Honey Slime


Honey Slimes are uncommon slimes that reside in The Moss Blanket. They can be easily identified by their golden-brown coloration, and the three-hexagon crest on their foreheads.

Hunter Slime


The Hunter Slime is the wild cousin of the tabby slime. A much more capable creature than the playful tabbies, the hunter slime excels at stalking the chickens of the Far, Far Range.

Puddle Slime


Puddle slimes are a rare species of slime that live in fresh water pools found on the range. These slimes don't eat fruits, veggies, or meat like most other slimes. Instead, puddle slimes absorb water.

Quantum Slime


At all times, the quantum slime is capable of emitting other possible realities of their being. Called 'ghosts', these other realities can come true, with the quantum slime aligning with the reality of the ghost, effectively teleporting.

Dervish Slime


A dervish slime is a swirling, whirling bundle of energy in slimy form. Dervish slimes are commonly found in the Glass Desert, cruising across its dusty dunes at high speed atop little whirlwinds.

Tangle Slime


Though the tangle slime may look innocent, it's actually a fearsome predator with a big appetite for tasty meat. The tangle slime earned its name from the twisting vines that it can produce to grab things in the environment.

Saber Slime


Though believed to be long extinct, the saber slime lives on through the largo slimes in The Wilds. Saber slimes were believed to be one of the fiercest predators during the Jellassic Period.

Harmful Slimes


These Slimes can harm the player in some way regardless of whether or not they are feral.

Rock Slime


Rock slimes earned their name from the rocky crown of sharp spikes atop their slimy bodies. Rock slimes are strict vegetarians, favoring the satisfying crunch of the heart beet most of all.

Boom Slime


Boom slimes are often heard before they're seen. The slime cells of a boom slime are constantly vibrating, causing their slime to increasingly crackle with energy before ultimately exploding.

Rad Slime


It's unknown if rad slimes, sometimes called 'raddies,' came to be from external radiation sources or from something naturally occurring within the Far, Far Range. Regardless, rad slimes emit such a potent aura of radiation they glow in the dark!

Crystal Slime


Believed to be a cousin of the rock slime, the crystal slime is covered in a crown of shimmering, crystal spikes. These spikes seem to form from the crystal slime generating a tremendous amount of internal heat and warping the minerals around them.

Fire Slime


Fire slimes are a rare breed of slime that lives on ash. Fire slimes will eat just about any food, but not without it being burned into ash first.

Mosaic Slime


The mosaic slime gets its name from the glittering array of glass tiles that cover its body. This glass produces a similar anomaly found in the desert itself, making the mosaic slime beautiful, but quite dangerous.

Tarr Slime


The tarr are formed when a largo eats a plort unlike the ones it produces. A terrible transformation occurs that turns the slime into a sludge filled with a swirling, prismatic array of colors and a relentless appetite.

Special Slimes


These slimes are a category of their own from the standard slimes.

Quicksilver Slime


Quicksilver slimes are among the rarest slimes on the Far, Far Range. Quicksilver slimes are lightning quick, and feed on electricity. This is generally accomplished by their moving really fast and slowly accumulating the static charge they generate.

Glitch Slime


Glitch slimes were formed from a bug in the codebase of Viktor's Slimeulation of the Far, Far Range. Glitch slimes do not seek out food like physical slimes.

Gold Slime


The most exclusive and sought after of all slime species is easily the gold slime. Very little is known about these slippery creatures as they have proven impossible to capture. Gold slimes will immediately begin to flee ranchers on sight.

Lucky Slime


Lucky slimes are a strange variant of tabby slimes that seem to have a fascination with shiny objects, particularly newbuck coins. However, coins don't make for a balanced diet and a lucky slime will still greedily devour meat of any kind.

Largo Slime


Largo Slimes are a hybrid of two slimes resulting from a slime eating a plort unlike its own. They are twice as big normal slimes and have a combined diet of their two source slimes, making them enticing for ranchers that want to maximize their profits.

Gordo Slime


Gordo Slimes are a rare phenomenon that occurs when multiple slimes of the same type congregate. The result is that the slimes seem to merge together into a much larger slime that is seemingly incapable of movement. A gordo will eat until it bursts.

Twinkle Slime


Few ranchers have ever even glimpsed a twinkle slime, with most believing it to be a legend. When they are found, twinkle slimes give away their location with the beautiful songs they sing. On approach they vanish from sight.
